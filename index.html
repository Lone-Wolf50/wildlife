FAQs

Humans can protect nature by reducing pollution, conserving energy, supporting wildlife organizations, and respecting natural habitats. Small actions, such as recycling and responsible tourism, make a big difference.

Oceans regulate the planet’s climate, produce a large portion of Earth’s oxygen, and support millions of species. Marine ecosystems like coral reefs are essential for biodiversity and coastal protection.

Animals adapt through physical features, behaviors, and survival strategies developed over time. These adaptations help them find food, avoid predators, and survive extreme climates such as deserts, oceans, and frozen regions.

Wildlife conservation protects endangered species and preserves biodiversity. Without conservation efforts, ecosystems can collapse, leading to climate imbalance, loss of habitats, and reduced resources for future generations.

An ecosystem is a community of living organisms interacting with their physical environment. It is important because every plant and animal plays a role in maintaining balance, supporting life, and sustaining natural resources like clean air and water.
